Giant Panda

The giant panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca), also known as the panda bear or simply panda, is a bear native to south central China. It is easily recognized by the large, distinctive black patches around its eyes, over the ears, and across its round body.

Though classified in the order Carnivora, the giant panda's diet is over 99% bamboo. In the wild, they may occasionally eat other grasses, wild tubers, or even small animals. In captivity, they might receive honey, eggs, fish, yams, or fruit.

Pandas live in a few mountainous regions in central China—mostly in Sichuan, and also in Shaanxi and Gansu. Due to farming and deforestation, they have been driven out of the lowlands. The giant panda is a conservation-reliant endangered species.
